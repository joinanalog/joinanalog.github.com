Our creative process

For the creation of these series we combine modern software techniques and ancient analog printing techniques to create beautiful physical notebooks rooted in digital data.

Digital Elevation Models
Our process starts with a research of the best elevations models available for the area we want to map.
Variety of sources
We get our models from multiple sources, including data by NASA, CGIAR-CSI and local institutions.
Isolines creation
Using open source libraries like GDAL we generate the isolines of the desired region in vector format.
Interaction opportunities
Thanks to the vectorial nature of our isolines we can integrate the contour lines in multiple contexts, like interactive, web-ready maps or printing high quality maps.
Manual editing
Fitting the hyper detailed isolines within the constrained canvas of a pocket notebook means a lot of manual editing to remove and simplify unnecesary points.
Attention to detail
We carefully review all the vector data to remove any strange artifacts (strange right angles, outliers, etc.) in order to get polished, accurate contour lines.
Final notebook
After a meticulous review of the design we print it using the best papers, sourced from sustainable forestry to create the perfect pocket notebook.
Letterpress cover
We enhance the finish of the notebooks with a letterpress cover, a tactile experience that match the represented nature.
